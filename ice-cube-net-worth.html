Ice Cube: Career

Ice Cube began rapping when he was 16 years old. With his friend Sir Jinx and K-Dee, he formed the rap group C.I.A.

In 1987, the rap group released My Posse, a track produced by Dr. Dre.

In the same year, he became a part of the freshly formed rap group N.W.A.

Music career

In the beginning, the public noticed him as the singer and lyricist of the N.W.A. band.

In 1989, Ice Cube composed N.W.A.’s debut album LP “Straight Outta Compton.” 3.5 million copies were sold. However, he only received $32,000.

In 1989, he left N.W.A. due to business disagreements as he earned less than $100,000.

After that, he began launching solo albums and gained success in no time.

Ice Cube’s earnings through those albums are: AmeriKKKa’s Most Wanted and Death Certificate” ($2.6 million), Kill at Will and 100 Miles and Runnin’ ($2,000,000), Murder Was the Case ($2,000,000), Bootlegs & B-Sides  ($500,000), War & Peace Vol. 1 & 2 ($2,000,000).

Cubes 1992 ‘The Predator‘ album was certified platinum two times by the RIAA and once by the BPI, and many others were also certified platinum.

According to estimates, each record earned more than $1 million, with Murder Was The Case and Friday making well over $2 million each.

Movie Career

He made his acting debut as Doughboy in Joy Singleton’s ‘Boyz n the Hood‘ (1991).

Regardless, the film was a great hit, grossing $57.5 million against a $6.5 million budget.

The next year, he played alongside Ice-T and Bill Paxton in the thriller Trespass. Cube also co-wrote and starred with Chris Tucker in the 1995 comedy movie “Friday.” It grossed $28 million worldwide and was followed by “Next Friday” and “Friday After Next.” In the 1998 film The Player’s Club, he made his directing debut. Following that, he authored and produced several films in which he acted as a protagonist or supporting character.

Ice Cube: Interesting Facts

  • He began creating raps in ninth grade at George Washington Preparatory High School in Los Angeles when a friend nicknamed “Kiddo” challenged him to do so in typewriting class. “Kiddo” lost.
  • He could have pursued a profession in architectural drafting if rap hadn’t worked out.
  • Cube has faced a “long, terrible history of anti-Semitism.”  His 1991 song “No Vaseline” had labeled N.W.A. members’ insults of blacks and called N.W.A’s manager Jerry Heller a “white man, Jew, demon, and cracker.”  In 2015, Cube apologized and clarified that the assaults were aimed at Heller and not “the entire Jewish race.”
