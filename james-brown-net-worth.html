James Brown | Net Worth & Music Rights

Net Worth

Musical icon James Brown’s net worth was $100 million at the time of his death in 2006.

Having started his career in the 50s, James practically ruled the R&B and soul music during his active years.

Over the course of his career, James managed to sell over 50 million records worldwide which earned him a sizable fortune.

Similarly, throughout his career, Brown released 15 live albums, 59 studio albums, 49 compilation albums, and 7 video albums.

Besides, he made a considerable amount from the music royalties and investments.

Likewise, his appearances in films and TV shows also aided in boosting his income.

Mr. Brown made a significant amount of money and lived lavishly.

However, the real drama began when he died and left his fortune without the proper will.

A lot of lawsuits were filed after his death on his music rights and estate.

The legal battle between his widow and nine children ran for years.

It took more than a decade to reach the closure of Mr. Brown’s estate.

As per the will, Brown left the majority of his wealth to underprivileged students in Georgia and South Carolina.

However, Brown’s only surviving wife, Tomi Rae Hynie, opposed the will, claiming that she was entitled to at least one-third of James’s estate and music rights.

Altogether, James’s wealth at the time was $100 million, including a music catalog, liquid assets, and future royalties.

However, the court determined that Tomi was never legally married to James, and she never annulled a previous marriage which made her claims invalid.

In the end, they all reached a compromise where James’s six surviving children and widow Tomi received ¼ of the Brown estate.

Music Rights

In 2021, December Primary Wave Music acquired James’s publishing rights. The deal was finalized at $90 million.

Well, even if the Primary Wave Music currently owns Brown’s music right, after a certain time, the rights will revert back to James’s children and his widow.

As per Federal copyright laws, after a certain time, the rights revert back to the artist or artist’s heirs, who can earn royalties from licensing.

It’s like renting the music for a certain amount of time.

In 2015, James’s widow sold a share of termination rights in just five songs which earned her $1.9 million.

However, the rights are now back to her.

James Brown | Lifestyle & Death

Lifestyle

James Brown was born in a poverty-stricken environment as his parents struggled financially. His family moved a lot and, at one point, ended up living in his aunt’s brothel.

However, as he got older and got engaged in music, his life changed dramatically. He had the repute of the hardest working man in the showbiz.

He always kept pushing himself to the limit. Brown had diabetes which remained undiagnosed for years.

Similarly, in 2004, he was diagnosed with prostate cancer. Moreover, James Brown was involved in several controversies over the course of his career.

He became sexually involved with Tammi Terrell after she joined the James Brown Revue.

She was just 17 at the time. However, the relationship continued until she ultimately freed herself from abuse.

Reportedly, Brown used to beat Tammi terribly until she bled.

Brown was not so much friendly and calm with his partners. Once, he reportedly kicked his pregnant girlfriend down the stairs.

This might be the reason he was married four times. His first wife was Velma Warren. Together they had one son.

Their divorce was finalized in 1969 after more than a decade of staying married.

A year later, he married his second wife, Deidre “Deedee” Jenkins in 1970. Similarly, he married Adrienne Lois Rodriguez in 1984 and Tomi Rae Hynie in 2002.

Moreover, Brown had numerous children, which makes sense given his marriage with four different women.

However, he only acknowledged nine of them, including five sons and four daughters.

According to some sources, Brown had at least three illegitimate children from his extra relationships.

During his last days, he resided in South Carolina.

Death

James Brown died at the age of 73 on Christmas Day 2006 at approximately 1:45 AM.

The musician died because of congestive heart failure and pneumonia complications.

However, in 2019, CNN and other journalists suggested that James had been murdered.

His memorial service was held at Apollo Theatre in New York.

Amongst the attendees were Michael Jackson, Dr. Dre, Prince, Lil Wayne, 50 Cent, Stevie Wonder, and LL Cool J, among others.

At the time of his death, James Brown’s net worth was a staggering $100 million.

James Brown | Charity

Despite his rape, domestic violence allegations, and other legal troubles, James always sought happiness in helping the underprivileged in society. Or at least he made believe he did.

Whatever the case was, he did benefit a few charitable organizations throughout his career.

After splitting his earthly possessions evenly among his six surviving children and widow, the remaining estate was to donate to “I Feel Good Trust” as per his will.

He founded the Annual James Brown Turkey and Toy Giveaways.

Through these events, James assisted the impoverished families and underprivileged children of the society.

After his death, his family established The James Brown Family Foundation in 2007 for educational and charitable purposes.

The foundation is headquartered in Clearwater, South Carolina.

James Brown | Career

Brown’s career began with a gospel group called The Gospel Starlighters.

The group later changed its name to The Flames.

They had a huge influence from artists like Ray Charles and Little Richard.

The group released their first album titled “Please, Please, Please” in 1956, which sold over a million copies.

Following the success of the album, James, along with the group, went on releasing several tracks. He also continued his solo career.

He slowly evolved his style towards funk.

Between the late 60s and early 70s, James immersed himself in the funk scene.
