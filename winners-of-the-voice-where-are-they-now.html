Winners Of The Voice Where Are They Now?

The Voice, NBC’s hit singing competition, has crowned many talented singers as winners since its inception in 2011. Each season, contestants vie for the title, a recording contract, and a cash prize, hoping to jumpstart their music careers. But what happens after the confetti settles? In this article, we’ll explore where the winners of The Voice are now, delving into their careers, achievements, and the paths they’ve taken since their respective victories.

Season 1: Javier Colon

Javier Colon, the inaugural winner of The Voice, captured audiences with his soulful voice. After his win, he released his album “Come Through For You” but eventually parted ways with Universal Republic Records. Colon continued to make music independently and has released several albums since, including “Gravity” in 2016. He also returned to The Voice to perform in later seasons and continues to tour and make music.

Season 2: Jermaine Paul

Jermaine Paul, a former backup singer for Alicia Keys, won the second season. He released his single “I Believe in This Life” but has yet to release a full album. Paul has kept a relatively low profile, performing at various events and working on his music independently.

Season 3: Cassadee Pope

Cassadee Pope, the first female winner of The Voice, has arguably become one of the show’s most successful contestants. She embraced her country roots and released her debut album “Frame by Frame” in 2013, which topped the country charts. Pope continues to record and perform, releasing multiple EPs and singles, and collaborating with other country artists.

Season 4: Danielle Bradbery

At just 16, Danielle Bradbery won Season 4 and quickly released her self-titled debut album. She has since become a mainstay in the country music scene, releasing albums like “I Don’t Believe We’ve Met” in 2017. Bradbery has also been nominated for several awards and continues to tour and release new music.

Season 5: Tessanne Chin

Tessanne Chin’s powerful voice led her to victory in Season 5. She released her album “Count on My Love” but faced challenges with its promotion. Chin has remained active in the music industry, performing internationally and releasing singles like “Love Suicide.”

Season 6: Josh Kaufman

Josh Kaufman’s win in Season 6 was a highlight of his career. He starred in the Broadway production of “Pippin” and continues to perform live. Kaufman has also released EPs and singles, showcasing his soulful voice and musical versatility.

Season 7: Craig Wayne Boyd

Craig Wayne Boyd’s country charm won over audiences in Season 7. He released his album “Top Shelf” in 2017 and continues to perform and write music. Boyd has also made appearances on various TV shows and remains active in the country music community.

Season 8: Sawyer Fredericks

Sawyer Fredericks, known for his folk-inspired sound, won Season 8 at just 16 years old. He has released several albums, including “A Good Storm” in 2016, and continues to tour and share his music with a dedicated fan base.

Season 9: Jordan Smith

Jordan Smith’s stunning performances on The Voice led to his Season 9 win. He released his debut album “Something Beautiful” and a Christmas album, both of which charted well. Smith continues to make music and has also ventured into songwriting for other artists.

Season 10: Alisan Porter

Alisan Porter, a former child star, won Season 10 and released her EP “I Come in Pieces” in 2017. She has focused on creating music independently and has released singles that showcase her powerful vocals and songwriting skills.

Season 11: Sundance Head

Sundance Head’s blend of country and soul won him Season 11. He signed with Republic Records and released his album “Stained Glass and Neon” in 2019. Head continues to perform and has a strong presence in the Texas music scene.

Season 12: Chris Blue

Chris Blue’s victory in Season 12 was marked by his energetic performances. He released singles like “Blue Blood Blues” and continues to work on his music while also serving as a worship leader and engaging in philanthropic efforts.

Season 13: Chloe Kohanski

Chloe Kohanski, now known as Chloe MK, won Season 13 with her distinctive rock voice. She has since rebranded and released alternative pop music, exploring a different direction from her performances on the show.

Season 14: Brynn Cartelli

The youngest winner of The Voice at 15, Brynn Cartelli won Season 14 and has since been developing her artistry. She released her single “Long Way Home” and continues to work on her debut album while finishing high school.

Season 15: Chevel Shepherd

Chevel Shepherd’s country roots led her to win Season 15. She has released singles like “Everybody’s Got a Story” and “Mama Got the Chair,” and is working on her debut EP while balancing her music career with her education.

Season 16: Maelyn Jarmon

Maelyn Jarmon’s classical training and unique voice won her Season 16. She has released singles post-show and is working on her debut album, aiming to create a sound that truly represents her as an artist.

Season 17: Jake Hoot

Jake Hoot’s country sound resonated with viewers, leading to his win in Season 17. He released his EP “Love Out of Time” in 2021 and continues to perform and release music, including a duet with Kelly Clarkson.

Season 18: Todd Tilghman

Todd Tilghman, a pastor and father of eight, won Season 18. He has since been working on his music career, performing live shows, and sharing his journey with fans on social media.

Season 19: Carter Rubin

At just 15, Carter Rubin won Season 19, becoming one of the youngest winners. He is currently working on his debut music while balancing his education and personal growth as an artist.

Season 20: Cam Anthony

Cam Anthony’s win in Season 20 showcased his versatile vocal abilities. He is currently working on new music and has performed at various events, including the National Christmas Tree Lighting ceremony.

Season 21: Girl Named Tom

Girl Named Tom, a sibling trio, made history as the first group to win The Voice in Season 21. They have been touring and working on original music, aiming to release an album that reflects their harmonious style.

Season 22: Bryce Leatherwood

Bryce Leatherwood’s classic country style won him Season 22. As the most recent winner, he is in the early stages of his post-show career but has expressed excitement about releasing music and performing for his growing fan base.

FAQ Section

  • How successful are The Voice winners?
    The success of The Voice winners varies. Some have gone on to have successful music careers, while others have faced challenges in the competitive industry. Many continue to make music and perform live.
  • Do winners of The Voice get a record deal?
    Yes, winners of The Voice typically receive a record deal as part of their prize package. However, the success of their subsequent releases can vary.
  • Has anyone from The Voice become famous?
    Several contestants from The Voice have achieved fame, with winners like Cassadee Pope and Danielle Bradbery becoming well-known in the country music scene.

In conclusion, the winners of The Voice have taken diverse paths since their victories. While some have found success in the music industry, others have faced challenges. Regardless, each winner has left their mark on the show and continues to pursue their passion for music. As The Voice continues to produce new talent, it will be interesting to see how future winners carve out their careers in an ever-evolving industry.
