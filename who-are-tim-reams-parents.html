Who are Tim Ream’s Parents? – Tim Ream is a prominent American professional soccer player renowned for his versatility in playing as both a center-back and left-back.

Born on October 5, 1987, he currently showcases his skills as a key player for Fulham, a Premier League club where he also serves as the vice-captain. Moreover, Timothy Ream is a valuable asset to the United States national soccer team.

Ream’s journey in soccer commenced during his youth and college years. Hailing from St. Louis, Missouri, he displayed exceptional talent during his high school days at St. Dominic. Ream’s impressive high school soccer career included being named a 2005 NSCAA All-Midwest Region selection. He continued to excel with a remarkable record of 15 goals and a school-record 39 assists.

His performances earned him numerous accolades, such as All-State, All-Conference, and Class 2 Defensive Player of the Year in both 2004 and 2005. Furthermore, he played a pivotal role in leading St. Dominic to the 2004 Missouri Class 2 state title. Ream’s passion for the sport extended to his participation with the academy club St. Louis Scott Gallagher, where he achieved the honor of winning national championships in 2003 and 2004.

Upon entering Saint Louis University, Ream’s soccer prowess continued to shine as he became a regular figure in the Saint Louis Billikens’ defense. His remarkable contributions earned him distinctions like NSCAA first-team All-Region and second-team All-Conference honors. Concurrently, Ream also showcased his talents with the Chicago Fire Premier in the USL Premier Development League.

In the 2010 MLS SuperDraft, New York Red Bulls drafted Tim Ream in the second round as the 18th overall pick. His exceptional performance during pre-season garnered admiration, especially from the team’s new coach, Hans Backe. Timothy Ream marked his professional debut on March 27, 2010, in New York’s opening game of the 2010 MLS season. His inaugural season with the Red Bulls was outstanding, as he started all 30 matches and contributed significantly to New York capturing its second regular season Eastern Conference title. At the season’s close, he was named the team’s Defender of the Year and was a top candidate for MLS Rookie of the Year.

During his tenure with New York, Tim Ream’s second season was marked by consistent performances. However, he missed several matches due to international call-ups for the 2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup. In the off-season of 2011, he briefly trained with West Bromwich Albion and Bolton Wanderers to maintain his fitness. In January 2012, Bolton made a transfer offer for him, which was accepted, and Ream joined the club on a three-and-a-half-year contract.

His time with Bolton Wanderers saw Tim Ream don the number 5 shirt and become an integral part of the team. Despite the club’s relegation from the Premier League, Ream continued to impress, making valuable contributions in defense. He also adapted to different positions, playing as both a left-back and center-back.

Tim Ream’s dedication and performance earned him accolades from the fans, as he was voted the fans’ player of the year and the club’s player of the year at the end of the 2013-14 season. He further solidified his commitment to Bolton by signing a new three-year contract ahead of the 2014-15 season. In the subsequent seasons, he consistently delivered solid performances, earning him consecutive Bolton player of the year awards.

In August 2015, Tim Ream’s career took a new turn as he joined Fulham. With Fulham, he demonstrated his versatility by playing as a center-back and left-back. His Fulham debut was marked by a 3-1 victory over Rotherham United, and he continued to be a crucial presence on the field.

Internationally, Ream represented the United States men’s national soccer team. He made his international debut against South Africa in 2010 and contributed to important matches in various tournaments, including the Gold Cup. After a brief hiatus, he returned to the national team in 2013 and continued to be a valuable asset.

In 2022, Timothy Ream’s international journey reached new heights as he was called up for the 2022 FIFA World Cup, where he played a pivotal role in the United States’ journey to the Round of 16. His dedication and performance on the global stage further solidified his reputation as a top-class soccer player.
